# Case-Derived Playbook

Use this file to turn strong public GPT-Image-2 examples into reusable production moves. Do not copy source prompts. Transfer the structure, visual logic, and anti-failure controls to the user's actual subject.

## How To Apply A Case Pattern

1. Identify the user's output category and business job.
2. Pick one primary pattern below. Add a secondary pattern only when it helps.
3. Replace every source-specific object, brand, headline, location, and style cue with user-specific details.
4. Preserve the production logic: layout, hierarchy, camera, lighting, material description, text control, and avoid rules.
5. In the final answer, include `Case pattern applied: [pattern name]` before the prompt.

## Product And E-Commerce Patterns

### Miniature Product Theater

Use for skincare, beverages, food, tools, appliances, packaging, and feature storytelling.

Transfer moves:

- Make the product oversized and central, with exact material, label, color, shape, and packaging.
- Build a miniature world around the product that metaphorically performs the benefit: workers crafting it, tiny chefs assembling ingredients, lab technicians testing purity, couriers delivering speed, gardeners growing natural ingredients.
- Use tilt-shift or macro product photography, clean studio background, realistic contact shadows, and commercial lighting.
- Keep the benefit legible through actions and props, not long text.
- Avoid product drift, random labels, toy-like low detail, clutter, and extra brand marks.

### Product TVC Storyboard Board

Use for e-commerce listings, video ad concepts, launch campaigns, and product education.

Transfer moves:

- Present exactly 6, 9, or 16 panels in a clean storyboard grid.
- Preserve one product identity across all panels.
- Define each panel as a shot: establishing, hero, macro craft detail, use case, benefit proof, cleaning or durability proof, scale/detail, mood/lifestyle, closing frame.
- Add timestamps, short scene titles, and concise notes in the requested language.
- Use one shared lighting and color system across all panels.
- Avoid wrong panel count, inconsistent product identity, unreadable captions, and random UI or logos.

### Food Hero Plus Keyframe Sheet

Use for restaurants, snacks, drinks, recipes, and food ad campaigns.

Transfer moves:

- Create one appetizing hero frame with texture, steam, gloss, crumbs, condensation, or sauce behavior.
- For motion or campaign output, add a keyframe sheet showing assembly, transformation, bite, pour, splash, or reveal.
- Use warm side light, shallow depth of field, controlled mess, and material-rich macro details.
- Keep labels minimal or omit text unless the user needs copy.

## Advertising And Luxury Patterns

### Luxury Hero Ad With Motion Energy

Use for watches, perfume, cars, electronics, fashion accessories, spirits, and premium products.

Transfer moves:

- Place the hero product in a dark or restrained premium environment with strong negative space.
- Use crisp specular highlights, reflections, backlight, rim light, streaks, smoke, water, metallic accents, or glossy ground planes.
- Add one oversized background word or short headline only when requested.
- Lock the palette to 2-4 colors tied to the product or brand.
- Describe brand marks only if supplied by the user; otherwise ban random logos.

### Isometric Luxury Model

Use for real estate, cities, campuses, venues, exhibition concepts, product ecosystems, and strategic maps.

Transfer moves:

- Treat the subject as a high-end miniature maquette on a visible base board.
- Use a high three-quarter isometric camera, centered hierarchy, precise zones, roads, paths, water, lighting, and scale markers.
- Combine warm practical lights with cool reflections for a premium planning-board feel.
- Keep the board edges visible so the image reads as a designed model, not generic scenery.

## Information, UI, And Document Patterns

### Whitepaper Information Atlas

Use for technical explainers, data visualization, science posters, education, strategy diagrams, and public-facing infographics.

Transfer moves:

- Start with a fixed page architecture: title area, central diagram or hero figure, side callouts, bottom legend/timeline/process.
- Use 3-6 modules only unless the user asks for dense detail.
- Pair short labels with visual evidence: arrows, cutaways, comparison bars, maps, flow lines, or component cards.
- Use professional editorial styling: whitepaper, museum board, technical magazine, or atlas plate.
- Make visible text short, large, and language-specific.

### UI Plus Social Screenshot Hybrid

Use for app launch visuals, social proof mockups, notebook pages, product screenshots, and platform-native posts.

Transfer moves:

- Combine a realistic device or platform frame with content cards, comments, metrics, and product UI.
- Lock the platform context: mobile screenshot, desktop dashboard, social post, messaging thread, or browser page.
- Use exact visible headline and section labels; keep microcopy sparse or simulated.
- Make the interface believable with spacing, icons, hierarchy, status bars, and interaction states.
- Avoid fake unreadable UI labels unless decorative text is acceptable.

### Editorial Or Publication Spread

Use for menus, magazines, newspapers, reports, catalogs, and document mockups.

Transfer moves:

- Define page format, columns, margins, headline placement, image slots, captions, pull quotes, and page furniture.
- Use exact short headlines and simulated body text for long copy.
- Keep typographic hierarchy restrained and readable.
- Anchor the design with one strong photo, illustration, chart, or artifact.

## Brand And System Patterns

### Brand Touchpoint Board

Use for identity systems, packaging systems, logo explorations, menu systems, social templates, and launch boards.

Transfer moves:

- Treat the output as a system board, not a single logo.
- Include brand strategy, palette swatches, typography samples, logo or mark variations, and 4-8 touchpoints.
- Show consistent visual DNA across packaging, card, screen, social post, label, signage, or merch.
- Use clear grouping and labels.
- Avoid unrelated styles between touchpoints, random wordmarks, and overcomplicated logos.

### Campaign Grid

Use for seasonal campaigns, fashion drops, sports campaigns, snack campaigns, and multi-image social packs.

Transfer moves:

- Use a fixed grid of cards or frames.
- Keep a shared campaign identity: palette, typography, prop language, lighting, pose, texture, and headline system.
- Vary subject pose, product angle, or scene role while maintaining one visual system.
- Add one campaign title and short per-frame labels if useful.

## Character, Portrait, And Culture Patterns

### Character Reference Sheet

Use for original characters, game assets, anime references, mascot design, toy concepts, and action pose sheets.

Transfer moves:

- Lock identity: face, proportions, silhouette, outfit, accessories, palette, and material.
- Use front/side/back views, expression row, action grid, or detail callouts.
- Specify exact grid count when using multiple poses.
- Keep a clean background and short labels so the character remains readable.

### Museum Or Cultural Plate

Use for historical objects, clothing, food, rituals, places, poetry, and regional culture.

Transfer moves:

- Use a museum board, field guide, atlas plate, archival catalog, or long-scroll educational layout.
- Center the artifact, figure, scene, or map; place callouts around material, structure, symbolism, process, and era.
- Use historically appropriate clothing, architecture, tools, and writing style.
- Ban modern objects, cosplay drift, generic fantasy, and e-commerce styling.

### Cinematic Portrait Variant

Use for editorial portraits, lifestyle campaigns, street photography, fashion, creator profile images, and mood portraits.

Transfer moves:

- Define camera feel, lens, framing, pose, expression, wardrobe, location, and light source.
- Use film stock, direct flash, neon, rim light, golden hour, rain reflections, or documentary texture only when relevant.
- Preserve facial identity from references if provided.
- Avoid plastic skin, distorted anatomy, and fake blur over the subject.

## Product Research And Technical Patterns

### Exploded Teardown Board

Use for electronics, furniture, industrial design, packaging, appliances, and concept products.

Transfer moves:

- Combine one final hero render with exploded components, material stack, dimensions, callouts, and 3-5 design evolution steps.
- Show inspiration-to-form translation when the user supplies a reference idea.
- Use technical drawing lines, CAD-like annotations, product photography, and clean board hierarchy.
- Keep labels short and readable.
- Avoid creating only a pretty product without structure or proof.

### Technical Mechanism Explainer

Use for RAG, AI systems, hardware flows, biological processes, manufacturing pipelines, and service workflows.

Transfer moves:

- Convert the system into modules, arrows, layers, input/output zones, feedback loops, and failure guards.
- Use one central architecture diagram plus 3-5 supporting cards.
- Keep text concise and operational: labels, verbs, numbers, and warnings.
- Avoid vague futuristic decoration without explanatory structure.
